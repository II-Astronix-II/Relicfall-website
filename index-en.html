Overview

Our story takes place in a distant village, known for the peace, joy, and unity that prevailed there. This village was once protected by powerful relics capable of repelling any monster attack. Alas, one day, it suffered the worst fate: the disappearance of the relics. This led to the fall of the village, which was later overwhelmed by relentless attacks from creatures, brutally ending an era of peace.

Soon after, traces of the missing relics were discovered in a mysterious dungeon that had recently appeared. Many adventurers rushed into it, hoping to conquer it and recover the lost relics, but none of them ever returned victorious.

Until your arrival in this faraway land.

Project presentation

Relicfall is a 2D top-down action-adventure game inspired by dungeon crawler and exploration games such as Zelda. Developed in Python using the PyGame library, the project was designed as a complete cooperative experience combining exploration, combat, progression systems, puzzles, and multiplayer gameplay within a dark fantasy universe. From the beginning of the project, the goal was not only to create a technically functional game, but also to build a coherent and immersive world capable of offering players a structured and evolving adventure. The game takes place across several interconnected dungeons filled with monsters, interactive mechanics, puzzles, merchants, and relics to recover in order to restore balance to the world.

Throughout the development process, the team progressively transformed the project from an initial technical prototype into a more complete and polished experience. The first stages focused mainly on the core foundations of the game, including player movement, dungeon loading systems, collisions, room transitions, menus, and the overall architecture of the engine. Once these technical bases became stable, the project expanded with additional gameplay systems such as enemy artificial intelligence, combat mechanics, progression systems, skill upgrades, lighting effects, merchants, multiplayer synchronization, and more advanced level design. Great attention was also given to atmosphere and immersion through pixel-art visuals, environmental design, sound integration, user interfaces, and progression pacing. Each dungeon was designed with its own mechanics, visual identity, and gameplay structure in order to create a varied and progressively more complex experience for the player.

Relicfall was also an important collaborative and educational experience for the entire team. Beyond the technical aspects of programming, the project required continuous organization, communication, adaptation, and teamwork in order to manage a large-scale development process alongside other academic constraints. The development involved many different fields including gameplay programming, artificial intelligence, level design, multiplayer networking, interface design, visual asset creation, sound research, and project presentation through a dedicated website. Despite time limitations and several ambitious features that could not be fully completed, the team successfully delivered a coherent and fully playable game representing the original vision of the project. Relicfall therefore stands both as a functional video game project and as a concrete demonstration of the group's ability to collaboratively design, organize, and develop a complex software project from conception to final presentation.

Project team

Relicfall was developed by a team of five students as part of the EPITA curriculum. Each member contributed to the project through specific areas such as gameplay, multiplayer systems, level design, user interfaces, visual assets, and sound design.

Eliott Averty

Development of the game's technical architecture, dungeon engine, LAN multiplayer system, save management, skills system, lighting effects, and a significant part of the user interface.

Rémi Jussac

Development of the core gameplay systems: player mechanics, combat, animations, collisions, enemies, artificial intelligence, progression systems, merchant interactions, and website development.

Augustin Sourisseau

Design of levels, lobbies, and progression mechanics, creation of visual assets, player sprites, environment props, and overall game environment structure.

Alexis Chapier

Creation of menus, control configuration systems, research and integration of music and sound effects, as well as several player-related graphical assets.

Odile-Anaelle Metsotsanga

Research and conceptual work related to the game's monsters, contribution to the creation of the bestiary, and consistency of the Relicfall universe.

Resources used

  • Python — Main programming language used for the project.
  • Pygame — Library used for the game engine, rendering, and interactions.
  • GitHub — Version control, collaboration, and website hosting.
  • Visual Studio Code — Development environment used during the project.
  • Aseprite — Creation and editing of pixel-art sprites and assets.
  • Freesound — Audio ambience and sound effect resources.
  • OpenGameArt — Visual assets and artistic inspirations.
  • Pixelify Sans — Font used for the website interface.
